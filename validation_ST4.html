Reflection on Validation

Using the W3C Validator was a massive reality check because it caught things I completely missed just by looking at the page layout. It pointed out skipped heading levels (e.g., going from h1 to h3 without an h2) and sections missing headings; stuff that technically worked on my screen but was breaking proper web standards under the hood.

Fixing those errors forced me to actually clean up my HTML structure. For my sitemap, I added a missing h2 heading. For my content page, I changed a <section> to a <div> because it didn't need a heading. This makes the whole site way more stable and accessible for things like screen readers.

It really showed me that just because a page looks good visually doesn't mean it's coded right and passing the official validation is what makes the difference between messy code and a professional, functional project.
